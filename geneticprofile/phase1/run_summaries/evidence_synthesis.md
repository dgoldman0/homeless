# Evidence Synthesis

The phase 1 interpretation is organized around a stable whole-genome frame plus a small set of local objects.

## Global Frame

The study target is Ashkenazi-centered at whole-genome scale. PCA/Q distance, control behavior, ROH context, regional proxy screens, and local-window sensitivity all support an Ashkenazi founder-history frame. Regional texture appears around Mediterranean, eastern-Mediterranean, West Asian, Baltic/Litvak, and Jewish-region axes, with source-level claims reserved for compact objects.

## High-Value Autosomal Objects

`chr10_lithuanian_tract` is the cleanest direct local tract. It has a 2.444 cM IBD/exact span, 75/75 selected markers, maternal-side coherence, and independent donor recovery through `lithuania3`.

`chr9_jewish_samaritan_core` is the strongest shared Jewish-region local object. It has a 95/95 exact core with `Samaritian988` and `ashkenazy8w`, plus anchor-switching behavior under donor removal. Roquetes medieval Jewish Catalonia data add broad/core chr9 context.

`chr3_rare_motif` is the strongest compact founder block. The complete seven-position bridge appears on one phased haplotype of the study target, with related carrier subclasses among `Jordan546`, `Adana23108.HO`, `HGDP00635.HO`, `Y064.HO`, `moroccoC61`, and `HGDP00672.HO`. Anchor validation shows the signal remains when either `Jordan546` or `moroccoC61` is removed and collapses when both are removed.

`chr3_belorus_micro_core` is a credible lower-tier local-Baltic micro-haplotype. It has a 27/27 exact core with `belorus5` and lacks threshold IBD support.

`chr16_regional_context` is context. It carries older regional texture without compact source-object support under current data.

## Decision Boundary

The current model is layered founder history. Broad external labels become evidence only when they produce compact, phase-aware, density-supported, control-stable local structure.
