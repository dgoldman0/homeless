# Analysis Guardrails

These guardrails summarize how evidence is weighed in the phase 1 interpretation.

## Evidence Priority

1. Phase-aware haplotype structure.
2. Parent-side coherence where available.
3. Direct IBD or exact-run support.
4. Marker density and boundary checks.
5. Ashkenazi-control and pseudo-target behavior.
6. ChromoPainter-style donor behavior and anchor-removal checks.
7. Sparse IBS ranks and broad proxy labels as context.

## Interpretation Rules

- Keep the Ashkenazi founder-history frame primary at whole-genome scale.
- Treat proxy labels as hypotheses and local structure as evidence.
- Keep direct maternal, direct paternal, and autosomal findings separate unless a future result supplies a bridge.
- Promote a window only when compact local evidence survives controls, phase/parent checks, and density checks.
- Use public ancient and historical datasets first as calibrated context.
- Carry sparse or null pilots as limits on current evidence.
- Treat outreach, account-gated waits, and controlled-access datasets as future triggers.
