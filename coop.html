Cebu Multipurpose Cooperative: Launch Plan

Business Model: Member-only restaurant, grocery, laundry, and convenience store. Non-members access via to-go/ordering only. 15+ founding members, with robust capital-build-up and profit-sharing structure. Targeting startup financing via Philippine government and SME lenders.

1. Entity Formation & Registration

1.1. Organize Founding Members & Capital

  • Minimum 15 natural-person members (RA 9520 compliance).
  • Paid-up share capital: ₱100,000 (minimum) (multipurpose cooperative).
  • Authorized capital: Recommend ₱1,000,000 (ensures no one member holds >10% of shares even if one person funds all paid-up capital).
  • Open co-op bank account or escrow and deposit initial paid-up capital. Obtain bank certificate.

1.2. Draft and Approve By-Laws

  • Capital build-up: Each member pays a ₱300/month “membership fee” credited 100% to share capital.
  • Patronage refund: At least 30% of net surplus is allocated annually to members, distributed proportionally to member purchases/usage; a % may be retained as additional shares.
  • Membership admission: New applicants must pass a knowledge test (mission/history) and demonstrate “goodwill” (e.g., volunteering, advocacy, etc.).
  • Set refund policy: Initial paid-up capital is refundable if startup loan is not approved (add explicit clause to subscription agreements and by-laws).

1.3. File with Cooperative Development Authority (CDA)

  1. Reserve co-op name via CDA portal.
  2. Submit Articles of Cooperation, By-laws, Treasurer’s Affidavit (with escrow certificate), Board Resolution, list of members, and IDs/specimen signatures.
  3. Receive Certificate of Registration as a multipurpose cooperative.

2. Capitalization & Funding Structure

2.1. Initial Equity

  • ₱100,000 paid-up share capital deposited in escrow/coop bank account (refundable per subscription clause if loan fails).
  • Monthly capital build-up dues increase equity over time.

3. Property & Fit-Out

  1. Identify and negotiate property acquisition/lease.
  2. Apply for loan using Certificate of Registration, business plan, financial projections, and escrow/hold-out deposit as initial security.
  3. On loan approval: mortgage property/equipment as required; begin build-out of restaurant, grocery, laundry, convenience store.

4. Member & Non-Member Participation

4.1. Member Benefits

  • Access to all facilities (dine-in, grocery, laundry, etc.).
  • Monthly share capital growth via dues and patronage refunds.
  • Voting rights and participation in governance and profit allocation.

4.2. Non-Member Access

  • Limited to to-go orders/services (restaurant, laundry, retail).
  • Revenues from non-member sales are taxable (VAT/percentage tax); maintain separate ledgers.

5. Membership Admission & Growth

  1. Require applicants to complete a knowledge test and a defined goodwill contribution (volunteer hours, advocacy, etc.).
  2. After 2 years’ audited operations, consider opening membership and scaling capital build-up.
  3. Allow portion of future patronage refunds to be retained as additional shares (“revolving capital”).

6. Compliance & Best Practices

  • Ensure ongoing compliance with CDA capital rules (₱100,000 paid-up; CBU in by-laws; ≤10% share cap).
  • Appoint auditor and prepare annual financial statements.
  • Disclose member equity statements annually (show share growth via dues and refunds).
  • File BIR returns, pay VAT/tax on non-member transactions.
  • Maintain open communication with all lenders and fulfill all post-approval requirements.
