# Literature Sources and Citation Roles

## Closest prior-art foil

### Garattini & Zatrimaylov — wormhole–warp-drive correspondence

- Remo Garattini and Kirill Zatrimaylov, “On the wormhole-warp drive correspondence,” *Journal of Cosmology and Astroparticle Physics* 2024(08):061, 2024.
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1088/1475-7516/2024/08/061
- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.15136
- Repository/record page: https://aisberg.unibg.it/handle/10446/287857

Citation role: this is the main foil for naive wormhole/warp hybrids. The paper makes it possible to embed a warp drive in a wormhole background by generalizing Natário–Alcubierre to nonzero intrinsic curvature, then finds that Alcubierre warp drives cannot traverse Morris–Thorne wormholes. The reported no-go leaves room for spherically symmetric, nonlocalized warp drives.

## Prepared infrastructure / route comparison

### Everett & Roman — Krasnikov tube

- Allen E. Everett and Thomas A. Roman, “Superluminal subway: The Krasnikov tube,” *Physical Review D* 56, 2100–2108, 1997.
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.56.2100

Citation role: closest infrastructure-side comparison. The Krasnikov tube addresses the Alcubierre bubble-wall control issue by preparing a route, while retaining negative-energy and chronology concerns.

## Warp-drive baseline and limitations

### Alcubierre — original warp metric

- Miguel Alcubierre, “The warp drive: hyper-fast travel within general relativity,” *Classical and Quantum Gravity* 11, L73–L77, 1994.
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1088/0264-9381/11/5/001

Citation role: original shift-driven warp bubble.

### Natário — zero-expansion warp drive

- José Natário, “Warp drive with zero expansion,” *Classical and Quantum Gravity* 19, 1157–1165, 2002.
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1088/0264-9381/19/6/308
- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/0110086

Citation role: clarifies that expansion/contraction is not the essential warp-drive feature; shift structure is central.

### Lobo & Visser — fundamental limitations

- Francisco S. N. Lobo and Matt Visser, “Fundamental limitations on ‘warp drive’ spacetimes,” *Classical and Quantum Gravity* 21, 5871–5892, 2004.
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1088/0264-9381/21/24/011
- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/0406083

Citation role: energy-condition and weak-field limitations for Alcubierre/Natário warp spacetimes.

### Bobrick & Martire — physical warp drives

- Alexey Bobrick and Gianni Martire, “Introducing physical warp drives,” *Classical and Quantum Gravity* 38, 105009, 2021.
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1088/1361-6382/abdf6e
- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.06824

Citation role: modern ADM framing of warp-drive spacetimes, including shell, capacity, lapse, and shift language.

### Fuchs et al. — constant-velocity physical warp drive

- Jared Fuchs, Christopher Helmerich, Alexey Bobrick, Luke Sellers, Brandon Melcher, and Gianni Martire, “Constant velocity physical warp drive solution,” *Classical and Quantum Gravity* 41, 095013, 2024.
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1088/1361-6382/ad26aa

Citation role: recent positive-energy/sub-luminal warp-drive comparison point. It addresses source feasibility rather than throat-rail handoff architecture.

## Wormhole baseline

### Morris & Thorne — traversable wormhole framework

- Michael S. Morris and Kip S. Thorne, “Wormholes in spacetime and their use for interstellar travel: A tool for teaching general relativity,” *American Journal of Physics* 56, 395–412, 1988.
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1119/1.15620

Citation role: baseline traversable throat, redshift, flare-out, and tidal-compatibility framework.

### Ford & Roman — quantum constraints

- L. H. Ford and Thomas A. Roman, “Quantum field theory constrains traversable wormhole geometries,” *Physical Review D* 53, 5496–5507, 1996.
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.53.5496
- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/9510071

Citation role: quantum-inequality pressure on wormhole source support.

## Controlled traversability mechanism

### Gao, Jafferis & Wall — traversable wormholes via coupling

- Ping Gao, Daniel Louis Jafferis, and Aron C. Wall, “Traversable wormholes via a double trace deformation,” *Journal of High Energy Physics* 2017, 151, 2017.
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/JHEP12(2017)151
- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/1608.05687

Citation role: controlled source/mechanism comparison in a holographic setting, distinct from macroscopic throat-rail engineering.
