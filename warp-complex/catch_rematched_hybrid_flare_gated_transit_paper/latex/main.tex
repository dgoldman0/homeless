\begin{abstract}
This paper presents a standalone reduced-engineering reference model for a composite faster-than-light spacetime architecture under the conditional assumption that the required null-violating or effective source support can be supplied.  The construction combines a hybrid flare-gated wormhole-support infrastructure with a catch-rematched throat-loaded transit packet.  The central design move is a passenger/infrastructure separation: the throat complex supplies capacity, lapse support, radial stretch, flare geometry, shift containment, transition timing, and source-current management, while passenger safety is attached to a finite protected packet worldtube.  The frozen candidate uses early-catch transition choreography, throat-gated shift, broadened mixed radial/angular capacity support, and moderate broad radial service stretch.  In the reduced ADM screens, transition-profile choices control the dynamic/current burden measured by $j_M$ and $K$, while capacity/support geometry controls the Hamiltonian/spatial-curvature burden measured by $\rho_H$ and ${}^{(3)}R$.  The confirmed candidate
\[
C_0=20,\quad C_\perp=5,\quad R_{\rm th}=1.25,\quad w_{\rm th}=0.12,\quad R_{\rm pass}=0.35,\quad w_{\rm pass}=0.08,\quad B_0=6,\quad w_B=12
\]
with transition parameters
\[
x_{\rm catch}=0.05,\quad x_\beta=0.70,\quad x_q=1.25,\quad w_{\rm catch}=0.25,\quad w_\beta=0.28,\quad w_q=0.30,\quad p_\beta=4
\]
clears packet and support-edge causal checks in the reported reduced screens.  Relative to the v01 geometry baseline, the candidate reduces the confirmed total source-demand proxy to about $0.162\times$, the geometry channel to about $0.274\times$, and the dynamic channel to about $0.0108\times$ on the three-scenario confirmation grid.  A conservative current-floor source screen reduces Lorentzian-sampled packet negative exposure at $V=10,\lambda=6,\tau=0.2$ to about $0.042\times$ the baseline.  Robustness checks preserve the mixed-capacity design family under pressure-sensitive proxy choices, score-weighting changes, and grid variation.  The paper's contribution is a concrete composite-design map: transition profiles manage current burden, mixed radial/angular capacity manages spatial-curvature burden, and packet safety is evaluated by the moving packet worldtube rather than by passive throat comfort.
\end{abstract}

\section{Purpose and main result}

Traversable wormholes, warp drives, Krasnikov-style route infrastructure, and ADM warp-shell models each isolate part of the faster-than-light engineering problem.  Wormhole work identifies throat support, flare-out, tidal, and chronology constraints.  Warp-drive work separates mobile shift, wall thickness, spatial capacity, and lapse/time-rate structure.  Prepared-route models emphasize infrastructure and causality control.  Controlled traversability work shows that source timing can make a channel traversable in special theoretical settings.  The present paper combines these lessons into a single reduced composite architecture.

The result is a packet-centered active-rail geometry.  The throat is treated as an active infrastructure plant.  The passenger is represented by a finite coupling packet.  The hard operating rule is
\begin{equation}
 \supp(\beta^l)\subseteq\supp(A,T),
 \qquad
 x_{\rm catch}<x_\beta<x_q,
 \label{eq:support_inclusion}
\end{equation}
with the operational sequence
\begin{equation}
 \hbox{catch} \rightarrow \hbox{fade shift} \rightarrow \hbox{relax throat}.
 \label{eq:catch_sequence}
\end{equation}
The passenger diagnostic is the moving packet norm,
\begin{equation}
 g_{\mu\nu}\dot{x}^{\mu}_{\pkt}\dot{x}^{\nu}_{\pkt}<0,
 \label{eq:packet_norm}
\end{equation}
with support-edge containment tracked separately by stationary and ADM diagnostics.

The frozen design is called the Catch-Rematched Hybrid Flare-Gated Transit v1.  Its design doctrine is
\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\hbox{early-catch transition choreography}
+
\hbox{broadened mixed radial/angular capacity support}
+
\hbox{moderate broad radial service stretch}
}.
\label{eq:doctrine}
\end{equation}

\section{Literature setting and specific gap}

The literature supplies the physical constraint map.  Morris and Thorne establish the traversable throat as a flared areal-radius minimum requiring exotic radial null support and low tidal forces for traversability \citep{morris_thorne_1988}.  Morris, Thorne, and Yurtsever connect wormhole engineering to chronology control when separated mouths are used \citep{morris_thorne_yurtsever_1988}.  Ford and Roman show that quantum inequalities impose severe magnitude-duration and length-scale pressure on macroscopic wormhole support, with negative energy tending toward thin regions or Planck-scale structures in the static cases they analyze \citep{ford_roman_1996}.  Fewster and Roman sharpen the null-contracted sampled-stress issue for small-exotic-matter proposals \citep{fewster_roman_2005}.  Dynamic-throat work keeps local null expansions and local energy-condition pressure central \citep{hochberg_visser_1998}.

Warp-drive work supplies the ADM and infrastructure vocabulary.  Alcubierre introduced a superluminal warp geometry with exotic source requirements \citep{alcubierre_1994}.  Pfenning and Ford connected the Alcubierre wall to quantum-inequality severity and extreme wall thinness \citep{pfenning_ford_1997}.  Van den Broeck showed that capacity engineering can change energy accounting by combining compact exterior structure with large interior volume \citep{van_den_broeck_1999}.  Natario showed that expansion/contraction is not the essential diagnostic of a warp-drive geometry, emphasizing the role of the shift structure \citep{natario_2002}.  Everett and Roman's Krasnikov-tube analysis emphasizes prepared infrastructure, causality, and negative-energy layers in route-based superluminal travel \citep{everett_roman_1997}.  Bobrick and Martire provide a broad ADM decomposition of warp-drive spacetimes into lapse, shift, spatial geometry, capacity, and time-rate roles \citep{bobrick_martire_2021}.  Recent positive-energy and subluminal warp-shell work reinforces the importance of matter-shell design and shift-vector engineering \citep{lentz_2021,fuchs_2024}.  Gao, Jafferis, and Wall show that traversability can arise from a controlled source-timing mechanism in a holographic setting \citep{gao_jafferis_wall_2017}.

The gap addressed here is design-specific.  The literature gives the principles: shell thickness matters, capacity matters, ADM role separation matters, prepared infrastructure matters, and source timing matters.  The present work supplies a concrete composite mapping:
\begin{align}
 \hbox{transition profiles} &\longrightarrow j_M,\ K,
 \label{eq:channel_dyn}\\
 \hbox{capacity/support geometry} &\longrightarrow \rho_H,
 \ {}^{(3)}R.
 \label{eq:channel_geom}
\end{align}
It also supplies a new capacity rule for this composite architecture: radial service capacity and angular capacity should be separated.  The angular sector is a spatial-curvature burden reducer in the packet-service geometry.  In the confirmed screen, the no-angular comparison $C_\perp=1$ leaves the geometry channel near the v01 baseline, while $C_\perp=5$ reduces the confirmed geometry cost to about $0.274\times$ and total cost to about $0.162\times$ relative to the v01 geometry baseline.

\section{Prior ingredients used by the composite model}

The composite design draws from three reduced design lines, all recast here as standalone ingredients.

\subsection{Hybrid flare-gated throat infrastructure}

The wormhole infrastructure uses a spherically symmetric metric family
\begin{equation}
 ds^2=-N(l,t)^2\dd t^2+B(l,t)^2\dd l^2+R(l,t)^2\dd\Omega^2.
 \label{eq:v1_metric}
\end{equation}
The infrastructure assignments are
\begin{align}
 B(l,t)&:\ \hbox{radial support dilution and service-coordinate stretch},\nonumber\\
 R(l,t)&:\ \hbox{flare/access-state and angular geometry control},\nonumber\\
 N(l,t)&:\ \hbox{timing, shoulder matching, and lapse margin}.\nonumber
\end{align}
The wormhole-only reference model uses a phase sequence
\begin{equation}
\hbox{$B$ prestretch} \rightarrow \hbox{$R$ flare opening} \rightarrow \hbox{quiet access hold} \rightarrow \hbox{$R$ closure} \rightarrow \hbox{repayment/shoulder matching} \rightarrow \hbox{$B$ reset}.
\end{equation}
Its reduced source architecture separates NMC-like support, infrastructure repayment, and shoulder/matching components.  In the wormhole-only closure, full-cycle flux-completed observer-family ratios clear unity for the monitored core, access, support, support-ring, and shoulder ledgers \citep{goldman_hybrid_flare_gated_paper}.

In the composite design, this wormhole infrastructure becomes active plant.  The passenger criterion moves to a protected packet.  The throat supports capacity, lapse, shift containment, release timing, and source-current management.

\subsection{Catch-rematched throat-loaded packet}

The original combined geometry introduced a throat-loaded ADM radial-axis form,
\begin{equation}
 ds^2=-T^2\dd t^2+A^2\left(\dd l+\beta^l\dd t\right)^2+A^2r(l)^2\dd\Omega^2.
 \label{eq:packet_metric_basic}
\end{equation}
The capacity and lapse support are paired,
\begin{equation}
 A=\exp(qW\ln C_0),
 \qquad
 T=\exp(qW\ln(\lambda C_0)),
 \label{eq:paired_AT}
\end{equation}
so that
\begin{equation}
 \frac{T}{A}=\lambda^{qW}.
 \label{eq:lapse_capacity_ratio}
\end{equation}
The shift is localized in the packet and gated by throat support,
\begin{equation}
 \beta^l(l,t)=-U(t)E(X(t))W(l)S(l-X(t)).
 \label{eq:shift_basic}
\end{equation}
The support-inclusion rule $\supp(\beta^l)\subseteq\supp(A,T)$ follows from the gated-shift screens: the independent passenger-shift branch generated lapse-shift failures, while the throat-gated branch cleared the tested traversal and exit configurations \citep{goldman_communication_paper}.

\subsection{Engineering screening framework}

The wormhole-support engineering framework decomposes a proposed spacetime plant into support, access, repayment, buffer, matching, transition, and source-realism functions, then assigns diagnostic gates to each function \citep{goldman_engineering_framework}.  The composite work applies that framework to a combined wormhole-warp system.  The source-support assumption is held fixed; the work reported here maps the engineering consequences of that assumption.

\section{Composite ansatz}

The final composite ansatz inserts the catch-rematched packet into the hybrid flare-gated throat controls:
\begin{equation}
 ds^2=-\alpha(l,t)^2\dd t^2+
 \gamma_{ll}(l,t)\left(\dd l+\beta^l(l,t)\dd t\right)^2
 +\gamma_{\Omega\Omega}(l,t)\dd\Omega^2.
 \label{eq:composite_metric}
\end{equation}
The fields are
\begin{align}
 \alpha(l,t)&=N_{\vone}(l,t)T_{\pkt}(l,t),\label{eq:alpha}\\
 \gamma_{ll}(l,t)&=B_{\vone}(l,t)^2A_\parallel(l,t)^2,\label{eq:gll}\\
 \gamma_{\Omega\Omega}(l,t)&=R_{\vone}(l,t)^2A_\perp(l,t)^2,\label{eq:go}\\
 \beta^l(l,t)&=-\dot{X}_{\rm coord}(t)E(X(t))W(l)^{p_\beta}S(l-X(t)).\label{eq:beta_final}
\end{align}
The packet uses mixed radial/angular capacity:
\begin{align}
 A_\parallel&=\exp(qW\ln C_0),\label{eq:Apar}\\
 A_\perp&=\exp(qW\ln C_\perp),\label{eq:Aperp}\\
 T_{\pkt}&=\exp(qW\ln(\lambda C_0)).\label{eq:Tpkt}
\end{align}
The coordinate speed is service-stretch aware,
\begin{equation}
 \dot{X}_{\rm coord}=\frac{U}{B_{\vone}(X)}.
 \label{eq:baware_speed}
\end{equation}
This conversion is an engineering requirement of the composite model: $B_{\vone}$ stretches radial proper length, so a proper-radial service speed must be translated into coordinate motion before it is used in the ADM shift.

The protected packet region is sampled as
\begin{equation}
 |l-X(t)|\le r_{\pkt}.
 \label{eq:packet_region}
\end{equation}
The stationary infrastructure diagnostic is
\begin{equation}
 g_{tt}=-\alpha^2+\gamma_{ll}(\beta^l)^2.
 \label{eq:gtt}
\end{equation}
The moving packet diagnostic is
\begin{equation}
 \mathcal{N}_{\pkt}=-\alpha^2+\gamma_{ll}\left(\dot{X}_{\rm coord}+\beta^l\right)^2.
 \label{eq:packet_norm_adm}
\end{equation}
The packet clears the causal check when $\mathcal{N}_{\pkt}<0$ across the sampled packet region.

\section{Diagnostics and cost functions}

The reduced ADM screens use the spatial metric $\gamma_{ij}$, lapse $\alpha$, shift $\beta^i$, and extrinsic curvature
\begin{equation}
 K_{ij}=\frac{D_i\beta_j+D_j\beta_i-\partial_t\gamma_{ij}}{2\alpha}.
 \label{eq:Kij}
\end{equation}
The Hamiltonian source-demand proxy is
\begin{equation}
 \rho_H=\frac{{}^{(3)}R+K^2-K_{ij}K^{ij}}{16\pi},
 \label{eq:rhoH}
\end{equation}
and the momentum/current source-demand proxy is
\begin{equation}
 j_M^i=\frac{D_j(K^{ij}-\gamma^{ij}K)}{8\pi}.
 \label{eq:jM}
\end{equation}
The reported norm is $|j_M|=(\gamma_{ij}j_M^ij_M^j)^{1/2}$.

The screens use two burden channels:
\begin{align}
 J_{\geom}&=\max|\rho_H|+0.05\max|{}^{(3)}R|,\label{eq:Jgeom}\\
 J_{\dyn}&=\max|j_M|_{\pkt}+\max|j_M|_{\edge}+0.02\max|K|_{\pkt},\label{eq:Jdyn}\\
 J_{\rm total}&=J_{\geom}+J_{\dyn}.\label{eq:Jtotal}
\end{align}
The exact weights are screening choices; robustness checks below confirm the family under alternative weights.  The source-exposure proxy is a conservative current floor,
\begin{equation}
 Tkk_{\rm floor}=\rho_H-2|j_M|.
 \label{eq:tkkfloor}
\end{equation}
A pressure-sensitive bracket was also checked using $\rho_H+\eta\rho_H-2|j_M|$ with $\eta\in\{-1,0,+1\}$ and a worst-sign bracket $\rho_H-|\rho_H|-2|j_M|$.

\section{Engineering interpretation}

The composite system shifts the engineering problem from a globally comfortable throat to an active packet-service apparatus.  The throat complex carries support machinery.  The packet carries the passenger criterion.  The diagnostics follow the object being protected:
\begin{align}
 g_{tt}<0&:\ \hbox{stationary infrastructure accessibility},\nonumber\\
 \mathcal{N}_{\pkt}<0&:\ \hbox{moving passenger/coupling packet safety}.\nonumber
\end{align}
This distinction explains why low-lapse reduced tests can trigger stationary $g_{tt}$ while the packet remains timelike under correct choreography.

The v1 design map is:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is a radial service-coordinate and support-smoothing actuator.
\item $R$ is a service flare and angular geometry actuator; the active-rail posture keeps $R$ open or delayed through service and relaxes it smoothly.
\item $N$ supplies timing and shoulder/lapse margin.
\item $W^{p_\beta}$ edge shaping cleans support edges after correct catch timing.
\item Early catch reduces dynamic current demand and deepens packet timelike margin.
\item Mixed radial/angular capacity reduces the spatial-curvature burden.
\end{enumerate}
The strongest new capacity statement is
\begin{equation}
 \boxed{\hbox{minimum useful radial capacity plus moderate angular capacity is favored over purely radial capacity.}}
 \label{eq:capacity_rule}
\end{equation}

\section{Conclusion}

This paper freezes a packet-centered composite wormhole-warp engineering reference model under the assumption that the required source support can be supplied.  The model uses hybrid flare-gated throat infrastructure as active rail plant and a catch-rematched throat-loaded packet as the passenger/coupling object.  The operating sequence is catch, fade shift, relax throat.  The support-inclusion rule is $\supp(\beta^l)\subseteq\supp(A,T)$.  The passenger criterion is the moving packet norm.

The source screen and robustness checks support a frozen balanced default with $C_0=20$, $C_\perp=5$, $R_{\rm th}=1.25$, $w_{\rm th}=0.12$, $B_0=6$, and $w_B=12$, plus a nearby pressure-leaning $C_\perp=3$ branch.
