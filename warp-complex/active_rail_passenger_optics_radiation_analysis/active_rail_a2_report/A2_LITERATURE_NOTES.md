# Literature Notes for Phase A2 Memo

## Miguel Alcubierre, 1994
The Alcubierre metric introduced the central warp-drive image: a compact passenger region carried by a shaped spacetime distortion. Its ADM form makes the shift vector central. For active-rail work, the key inheritance is not the moving bubble itself but the separability of transport role from ordinary acceleration.

Reference:
- Miguel Alcubierre, “The warp drive: hyper-fast travel within general relativity,” Classical and Quantum Gravity 11, L73-L77 (1994).
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1088/0264-9381/11/5/001
- arXiv mirror: https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/0009013

## Clark, Hiscock, and Larson, 1999
This paper directly motivates viewport analysis. It integrates null geodesics in Alcubierre spacetime and studies angular deflection/redshift for photons reaching an observer in the bubble. The finding of aberration and horizon-like structures makes it clear that “what passengers see” must be computed through ray maps, not guessed from external scenery.

Reference:
- Chad Clark, William A. Hiscock, Shane L. Larson, “Null geodesics in the Alcubierre warp drive spacetime: the view from the bridge.”
- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/9907019

## Everett and Roman, 1997
The Krasnikov tube shifts the control issue toward prepared infrastructure. The paper emphasizes that an observer at the center of an Alcubierre bubble is causally separated from the outer wall and cannot create/control it on demand. This supports the active-rail instinct that the hard geometry should live in infrastructure rather than in the passenger vehicle.

Reference:
- Allen E. Everett and Thomas A. Roman, “A Superluminal Subway: The Krasnikov Tube,” Physical Review D 56, 2100 (1997).
- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/9702049

## Finazzi, Liberati, and Barceló, 2009
This is a central caution. Dynamical superluminal warp drives can produce Hawking-like flux for central observers and RSET growth near the front wall. A2 does not address this. Any serious radiation-safety claim must eventually confront RSET/semiclassical behavior.

Reference:
- Stefano Finazzi, Stefano Liberati, Carlos Barceló, “Semiclassical instability of dynamical warp drives,” Physical Review D 79, 124017 (2009).
- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/0904.0141

## McMonigal, Lewis, and O’Byrne, 2012
This paper motivates particle pickup/release testing. It studies null and massive particles interacting with Alcubierre bubbles and finds high-energy behavior and release hazards. Active-rail work should test whether throat-contained shift avoids or relocates these hazards.

Reference:
- Brendan McMonigal, Geraint F. Lewis, Philip O’Byrne, “The Alcubierre Warp Drive: On the Matter of Matter.”
- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/1202.5708

## Bobrick and Martire, 2021
Their “physical warp drives” framework is useful because it treats warp geometries through ADM-like design handles and physical conditions rather than a single indivisible bubble image. The active rail follows this engineering style by assigning capacity, lapse/time-rate support, and shift to the throat plant.

Reference:
- Alexey Bobrick and Gianni Martire, “Introducing physical warp drives,” Classical and Quantum Gravity 38, 105009 (2021).
- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.06824
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1088/1361-6382/abdf6e

## Garattini and Zatrimaylov, 2024
This paper formalizes a wormhole--warp-drive correspondence and identifies obstructions for localized warp-drive structures in wormhole backgrounds. It is a key reason to distinguish the active rail from “a warp bubble passing through a throat.” The active rail relocates the transport shift into the throat support envelope.

Reference:
- Remo Garattini and Kirill Zatrimaylov, “On the Wormhole--Warp Drive Correspondence,” JCAP 2024.
- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.15136
