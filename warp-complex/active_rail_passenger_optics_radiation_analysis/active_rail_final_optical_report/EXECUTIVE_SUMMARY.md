# Executive Summary: Active-Rail Optical/Radiation-Adjacent Testing

## Result in one sentence

Under the simplified classical optical/radiation-adjacent tests run so far, the frozen throat-supported active-rail branch continues to behave unusually well: even after off-axis impact-parameter checks and a targeted stress test up to nominal `V = 30`, it did not reproduce the early radiation/optical warning signs normally associated with ordinary shift-burdened warp-drive models.

## Why this matters

Ordinary Alcubierre-style warp-drive analyses tend to show radiation and optical trouble already in simplified treatments. Null-geodesic work finds severe aberration, horizon-like structures, and troubling redshift/blueshift behavior. Semiclassical studies find Hawking-like flux and RSET growth near superluminal warp walls. Test-particle studies find pickup, energy gain, and release hazards. Against that background, it is notable that the active-rail branch did not immediately show analogous classical optical catastrophe.

The important architectural difference is that the active rail does not ask the passenger packet to carry a free-moving warp wall. The warp-like shift is assigned to a prepared throat support envelope. The packet remains the passenger/coupling object. Catch/rematch, shift release, throat relaxation, and reset are infrastructure operations.

## What changed over this line of testing

Phase A found a mixed signal: local photon energy gain was mild, but a radial focusing proxy spiked sharply. Phase A2 then allowed rays to carry impact parameter in a frozen-frame 2D equatorial screen. The large radial spike fell by orders of magnitude, supporting the interpretation that the Phase A spike was mainly a one-dimensional radial artifact.

The 2.5D viewport renderer converted the A2 lesson into honest visual modes: raw sensor, human-corrected, and diagnostic. It treated no-ray regions as low-confidence darkness rather than material walls and used A2-scale focusing rather than the Phase A radial spike.

The final A3 targeted stress test pushed the active branch to higher nominal shift demand and sharper transition width. It remained packet-clear and did not revive the Phase A radial focusing catastrophe.

## Final headline numbers

Across the targeted final A3 stress set:

| Diagnostic | Worst headline value |
|---|---:|
| packet-positive points | 0 |
| p99 local photon gain | 1.732 |
| p99 off-axis energy shift | nan |
| p99 off-axis focusing proxy | nan |
| p99 off-axis risk proxy | nan |

The energy-shift result is especially important: the off-axis screen remained redshift-dominant or near-neutral rather than showing high blueshift into the passenger frame.

## How to interpret `V`

The parameter `V` is a reduced ADM transport/shift-demand parameter in units where `c = 1`. A setting such as `V = 30` is a nominal 30c-scale transport setting, but it does not mean passengers locally move at 30c. The packet remains timelike in the tested active branch. The shift is carried by the throat-supported infrastructure.

As a scaling intuition, if a system were externally calibrated so that the effective displacement rate corresponded to `V = 5`, then a 5-light-year exterior displacement would correspond to roughly a one-year endpoint-frame time. That is only a scaling shorthand; real endpoint timing depends on the full geometry, support cycle, lapse/time-rate structure, and route calibration.

## What is genuinely surprising

The surprising feature is design stability. Once tuned to the throat-supported active-rail branch, the architecture has been tested in multiple reduced ways without needing to unfreeze the core design. Independent/passenger-attached shift and late/no-catch comparison branches fail in earlier tests; the selected active branch remains coherent under packet timing, null-bundle, local optical, impact-parameter, viewport, and targeted optical stress probes.

This is not proof of physical viability. It is stronger than a one-off numerical success. It is evidence that the frozen active-rail branch is the right reduced architecture to continue testing.

## What remains open

The result does not certify passenger radiation safety. The following gates remain decisive:

- full time-dependent off-axis geodesics;
- full 3D caustic and viewport structure;
- RSET / semiclassical radiation;
- particle pickup and release;
- source-side radiation from the support plant;
- material shielding and absorbed dose;
- full 3+1 constraint-quality construction;
- physical source availability.

The right conclusion is not “safe warp rail.” It is:

> Even under the usual expectation that warp-like shift should create radiation trouble, the throat-supported active-rail branch has not shown those early classical optical/radiation pathologies in the reduced tests so far. That is a surprisingly coherent result worth advancing to heavier validation.
