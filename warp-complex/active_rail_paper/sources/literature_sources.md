# Literature source notes

This bundle uses sources that were checked during the session and cites them in the LaTeX bibliography.

## Historical motivation

- Daniel S. Goldman, *An Inquiry on Warp Drives and Wormholes* (2022), DOI `10.31219/osf.io/sre5z`. The public ResearchGate page identifies the June 2022 preprint, the DOI, and the core question about combining warp drives and wormholes.

## Closest formal foil

- Remo Garattini and Kirill Zatrimaylov, *On the wormhole-warp drive correspondence*, JCAP 2024(08), 061 (2024), DOI `10.1088/1475-7516/2024/08/061`, arXiv `2401.15136`. The public abstract states the correspondence between Morris-Thorne wormholes and warp-drive metrics, the need to generalize Natario-Alcubierre by introducing nonzero intrinsic curvature, and the result that Alcubierre warp drives cannot traverse Morris-Thorne wormholes, with a nonlocalized spherical exception.

## Foundational wormhole constraints

- Morris and Thorne (1988) for traversable-throat framing.
- Morris, Thorne, and Yurtsever (1988) for chronology/time-machine issues.
- Ford and Roman (1996) and Fewster and Roman (2005) for quantum-inequality pressure on wormhole support.
- Hochberg and Visser (1998) for dynamic wormhole NEC pressure.

## Warp-drive and prepared-route constraints

- Alcubierre (1994), Pfenning and Ford (1997), Natario (2002), Van den Broeck (1999), Bobrick and Martire (2021), and Fuchs et al. (2024) for warp-drive geometry, source, ADM, capacity, and matter-shell context.
- Everett and Roman (1997) for Krasnikov tubes and the bubble-wall control problem.
- Finazzi, Liberati, and Barcelo (2009) for semiclassical instability pressure in dynamical superluminal warp drives.
- Gao, Jafferis, and Wall (2017) for controlled traversability through a specific quantum/holographic source mechanism.
- Chowdhury (2025) for a recent extension of Alcubierre-Natario warp drives in non-flat Martel-Poisson chart backgrounds.
