# Source-History Screen Notes

The old adiabatic screen emphasized peak dynamic diagnostics such as `|B_t/B|`, `|B_tt/B|`, and flux. The stricter screen emphasizes what support observers sample over the full history.

The important distinction is:

```math
\text{peak dynamic cost}\downarrow \quad \not\Rightarrow \quad \text{sampled negative history}\downarrow.
```

In the B-only model, slower ramps reduce peak dynamic budgets but increase the time over which the always-on areal throat carries negative null-contracted support.

In the flare-gated branch, B setup/reset happens while the R-flare is flattened. This removes nearly all core negative exposure from the long B ramps. The remaining source-history burden is concentrated in R-open, hold, and R-close.

This is a control and geometry improvement. It does not replace the quantum source-realism gate.
