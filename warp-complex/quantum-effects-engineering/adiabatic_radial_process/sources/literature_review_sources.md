# Literature Review Sources

This source note records the primary literature anchors used in the standalone memo. It is a compact source guide rather than a complete bibliography.

## Traversable wormhole throat and exotic support

- Michael S. Morris and Kip S. Thorne, “Wormholes in spacetime and their use for interstellar travel: A tool for teaching general relativity,” *American Journal of Physics* 56, 395–412 (1988). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1119/1.15620.
  - Baseline traversable-throat construction and flare-out support language.

## Quantum-inequality constraints and quantum interest

- L. H. Ford and Thomas A. Roman, “Quantum field theory constrains traversable wormhole geometries,” *Physical Review D* 53, 5496–5507 (1996). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.53.5496.
  - Quantum-inequality pressure on macroscopic wormholes and length-scale discrepancy framing.
- L. H. Ford and Thomas A. Roman, “The quantum interest conjecture,” *Physical Review D* 60, 104018 (1999). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.60.104018.
  - Negative energy/positive repayment timing and overcompensation constraints.

## Small-exotic-matter and null-contracted stress tests

- Matt Visser, Sayan Kar, and Naresh Dadhich, “Traversable Wormholes with Arbitrarily Small Energy Condition Violations,” *Physical Review Letters* 90, 201102 (2003). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.90.201102.
  - Volume-integral quantifier and small total energy-condition violation.
- Sayan Kar, Naresh Dadhich, and Matt Visser, “Quantifying energy condition violations in traversable wormholes,” *Pramana* 63, 859–864 (2004). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/BF02705207.
  - Follow-on volume-integral framing.
- Christopher J. Fewster and Thomas A. Roman, “On wormholes with arbitrarily small quantities of exotic matter,” *Physical Review D* 72, 044023 (2005). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.72.044023.
  - Null-contracted stress averaged over timelike worldlines, applied to VKD and Kuhfittig-style models.

## Dynamic and evolving wormholes

- David Hochberg and Matt Visser, “The Null Energy Condition in Dynamic Wormholes,” *Physical Review Letters* 81, 746–749 (1998). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.81.746.
  - Local dynamic-throat and null-congruence formulation.
- Peter K. F. Kuhfittig, “Static and dynamic traversable wormhole geometries satisfying the Ford-Roman constraints,” *Physical Review D* 66, 024015 (2002). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.66.024015.
  - Static/dynamic wormholes under Ford–Roman constraints; dynamic cases retain WEC pressure.
- Sayan Kar and Deshdeep Sahdev, “Evolving Lorentzian Wormholes,” *Physical Review D* 53, 722–730 (1996). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.53.722.
  - Evolving Lorentzian wormholes with scale-factor-style time dependence.

## Metric engineering context

- Alexey Bobrick and Gianni Martire, “Introducing physical warp drives,” *Classical and Quantum Gravity* 38, 105009 (2021). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1088/1361-6382/abdf6e.
  - ADM role separation and metric variables as engineering controls in adjacent exotic-spacetime infrastructure work.
