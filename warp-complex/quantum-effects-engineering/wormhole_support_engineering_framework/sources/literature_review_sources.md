# Literature review source notes

This file records the primary source anchors used for the literature review and bibliography. The report uses the DOI/bibliographic records rather than downloaded paywalled PDFs.

## Traversable wormhole throat and energy conditions

- Morris, M. S. and Thorne, K. S. (1988). "Wormholes in spacetime and their use for interstellar travel: A tool for teaching general relativity." American Journal of Physics 56, 395-412. DOI: 10.1119/1.15620.
  - Role in report: baseline traversable wormhole throat, flare-out condition, radial tension/exotic stress language.

- Morris, M. S., Thorne, K. S., and Yurtsever, U. (1988). "Wormholes, time machines, and the weak energy condition." Physical Review Letters 61, 1446-1449. DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevLett.61.1446.
  - Role in report: chronology and weak-energy-condition context for wormhole-like infrastructure.

- Visser, M. (1995). Lorentzian Wormholes: From Einstein to Hawking. American Institute of Physics.
  - Role in report: broader wormhole energy-condition and construction context.

## Quantum inequalities and quantum interest

- Ford, L. H. and Roman, T. A. (1996). "Quantum field theory constrains traversable wormhole geometries." Physical Review D 53, 5496-5507. DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevD.53.5496.
  - Source record reviewed: APS abstract and OSTI metadata. OSTI summary records the core conclusion: macroscopic traversable wormholes require either near-Planck scale throats or large length-scale discrepancies; in the latter case negative energy is typically concentrated in a thin band relative to throat size.
  - Role in report: sampled-energy/length-scale gate.

- Ford, L. H. and Roman, T. A. (1999). "The quantum interest conjecture." Physical Review D 60, 104018. DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevD.60.104018.
  - Source record reviewed: APS abstract. The abstract states that negative energy pulses require compensating positive pulses, with temporal separation inversely related to amplitude.
  - Role in report: repayment and source-management gate.

- Fewster, C. J. and Teo, E. (1999). "Bounds on negative energy densities in static space-times." Physical Review D 59, 104016. DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevD.59.104016.
  - Source record reviewed: APS and York Research Database records. The work derives static-spacetime quantum inequalities for scalar fields measured by static observers.
  - Role in report: observer/sampling discipline.

- Fewster, C. J. and Roman, T. A. (2005). "On wormholes with arbitrarily small quantities of exotic matter." Physical Review D 72, 044023. DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevD.72.044023.
  - Source record reviewed: APS and York records. The abstract states that null-contracted stress averaged over timelike worldlines constrains general wormhole models; VKD models are constrained to submicroscopic size or large throat/curvature-radius discrepancy; a Kuhfittig model is nontraversable because light rays and particles require infinite affine parameter to reach the throat.
  - Role in report: source-realism, null-contracted QI, and traversal/affine gates.

## Small integrated exotic matter and slow-flare routes

- Visser, M., Kar, S., and Dadhich, N. (2003). "Traversable Wormholes with Arbitrarily Small Energy Condition Violations." Physical Review Letters 90, 201102. DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevLett.90.201102.
  - Source record reviewed: APS abstract. The work develops a measure for total energy-condition violation and demonstrates geometries supported by arbitrarily small amounts under that volume-integral measure.
  - Role in report: local/integrated-burden separation gate.

- Kar, S., Dadhich, N., and Visser, M. (2004). "Quantifying energy condition violations in traversable wormholes." Pramana 63, 859-864. DOI: 10.1007/BF02705207.
  - Source record reviewed: Springer abstract. The work discusses quantifying violation through volume integrals.
  - Role in report: integrated burden gate.

- Kuhfittig, P. K. F. (2002). "Static and dynamic traversable wormhole geometries satisfying the Ford-Roman constraints." Physical Review D 66, 024015. DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevD.66.024015.
  - Source record reviewed: APS abstract. The dynamic analysis finds weak-energy-condition violation unlikely to be avoidable even temporarily.
  - Role in report: static/dynamic Ford-Roman-compatible benchmark and dynamic gate.

- Zaslavskii, O. B. (2007). "Traversable wormholes: Minimum violation of the null energy condition revisited." Physical Review D 76, 044017. DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevD.76.044017.
  - Source record reviewed: APS abstract. The abstract states that zeroing exotic material near the throat can lead to horn-like behavior or horizon-approach with large stresses.
  - Role in report: caution for small-integrated-burden and slow-flare gates.

## Dynamic throat and numerical exotic-spacetime engineering

- Hochberg, D. and Visser, M. (1998). "Dynamic wormholes, antitrapped surfaces, and energy conditions." Physical Review D 58, 044021. DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevD.58.044021.
  - Source record reviewed: APS abstract. The work develops a local throat definition based on marginally anti-trapped surfaces and finds NEC violation generic at dynamic wormhole throats.
  - Role in report: dynamic null-expansion gate.

- Pfenning, M. J. and Ford, L. H. (1997). "The unphysical nature of 'warp drive'." Classical and Quantum Gravity 14, 1743-1751. DOI: 10.1088/0264-9381/14/7/011.
  - Role in report: quantum-inequality warning for exotic-spacetime engineering.

- Bobrick, A. and Martire, G. (2021). "Introducing physical warp drives." Classical and Quantum Gravity 38, 105009. DOI: 10.1088/1361-6382/abdf6e.
  - Source record reviewed: Monash/Scixplorer records. The work presents a general warp-drive spacetime model and separates controllable space capacity and time rate.
  - Role in report: engineering role separation for metric functions.

- Helmerich, C., Fuchs, J., Bobrick, A., Sellers, L., Melcher, B., and Martire, G. (2024). "Analyzing warp drive spacetimes with Warp Factory." Classical and Quantum Gravity 41, 095009. DOI: 10.1088/1361-6382/ad2e42.
  - Source record reviewed: Monash record. The work introduces a numerical toolkit for warp-drive spacetimes that evaluates the Einstein equations, energy conditions, metric scalars, and visualizations.
  - Role in report: comparison point for numerical exotic-spacetime screening.

- Fuchs, J., Helmerich, C., Bobrick, A., Sellers, L., Melcher, B., and Martire, G. (2024). "Constant velocity physical warp drive solution." Classical and Quantum Gravity 41, 095013. DOI: 10.1088/1361-6382/ad26aa.
  - Source record reviewed: Monash record. The abstract reports a numerical constant-velocity subluminal warp-drive solution with a stable matter shell satisfying energy conditions.
  - Role in report: engineering-adjacent metric construction and screening context.
